Motivation

Micro-credentials are scaling.
Our understanding of engagement is not.

Platforms know who logged in and who completed — but they rarely reveal how teachers engaged, what patterns emerged, and which learning processes actually unfolded.

Theoretical Framework

Engagement is multidimensional — and measurable from data

🧑‍🏫
Adult Learning Theory (Knowles, 2013)
Teachers are self-directed learners driven by autonomy, relevance, and experience — online platforms must support this
📐
Multidimensional Engagement (Finn & Zimmer, 2012)
Engagement = cognitive (investment) + behavioral (actions) + emotional (affect) + social (interaction)
📉
The Proxy Problem (Hershkovitz et al., 2025)
Prior work over-relies on click counts and logins — behavioral proxies that do not reflect genuine learning
🔬
Learning Analytics as Solution
Digital trace data can empirically reveal hidden engagement dimensions — moving beyond surface metrics to model the true structure of participation
Research Questions

Three questions, one integrated study

RQ1 What latent dimensions of teacher engagement are reflected in digital trace data?
RQ2 To what extent do engagement dimensions predict learning outcomes — course completion, activity completion, and practical application?
RQ3 What distinct profiles of teacher engagement emerge from the data, and what are their characteristics?

Engagement Behavior Variables

Outcome Variables

Finding 3 · Cluster Analysis

Three distinct teacher engagement profiles

🤝 Active Collaborators
High discussion, average performance
↑ Forum activity (+1.01) ↑ Learning duration (+1.11) ≈ Quiz score (avg)
🏃 Struggling Strivers
High effort, low achievement
↑↑ Log records (+0.88) ↑↑ Learning duration (+1.92) ↓ Quiz score (–0.75)
⚡ Efficient Achievers
Low activity, high performance
↓ Log records (lowest) ↓ Forum activity (below avg) ↑↑ Quiz score (+0.78)
Key insight Observable effort is not a reliable proxy for achievement — high-effort learners may still underperform without adequate support.
Discussion

What do these patterns tell us?

  • Forum = social learning = completion. Discussion participation is the single strongest lever — far outweighing quiz performance
  • "Efficient Achievers" bypass forums yet succeed — likely through prior expertise, offline reflection, or external resources
  • "Struggling Strivers" invest significant time and effort but underperform → need adaptive scaffolding & timely feedback
  • Challenges adult learning theory's collaborative ideal: some high-achievers effectively withdraw from the learning community
Learning Sciences insight Some important learning processes may occur outside the logs — offline reflection, prior knowledge activation — not visible in behavioral data.
Design challenge How do we leverage forum participation without coercing it from learners who don't need it?
Implications & Next Steps

What should we do with this?

🎓 Design Implications
  • Prioritize meaningful discussion design in online TPD — it drives completion
  • Develop adaptive feedback loops for high-effort, low-achievement learners
  • Rethink completion metrics: move beyond activity counts toward quality of engagement
🔭 Future Research
  • Expand to the full Hakaveret dataset (839 courses) to test stability of the 3-factor structure
  • NLP analysis of forum content — measure quality of discourse, not just quantity
  • Multimodal learning analytics: self-reports + classroom observation
📐 Methodological Contribution
  • Replicable multi-method framework: factor analysis + regression + clustering
  • Moves beyond behavioral proxies to empirically grounded engagement dimensions
⚠️ Limitations
  • Initial stage — 8 courses from one platform
  • Behavioral trace data cannot fully capture cognitive or emotional engagement
  • Forum quantity ≠ forum quality
Conclusion

A data-driven picture of how teachers engage

Three Dimensions
Forum Activity, Quiz Performance, and Learning Effort are the empirically derived building blocks of teacher engagement in micro-credentials
Forum Drives Completion
Social participation in discussions is 15× more predictive of course completion than other engagement behaviors
Three Teacher Profiles
Active Collaborators, Struggling Strivers, and Efficient Achievers — each requiring different platform support and design responses
The Big Takeaway
Observable activity is not a reliable proxy for learning. Platform design must foster discussion, not just track clicks
